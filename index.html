💡 Key Insights

01
MARKET SHARE
Blinkit Leads, Zepto Closes Fast
Blinkit holds 51% share driven by variety. Zepto captures 34% with price-conscious users. Instamart at just 6% — Swiggy's cross-sell opportunity missed.
51% Blinkit dominance
02
DECISION FACTOR
Price Is King — Speed Is Overrated
36% choose apps based on price, only 15% on speed. The 10-minute delivery war? Consumers don't care as much as companies think.
36% choose price first
03
PARADOX
The "Satisfied But Frustrated" Paradox
72% are satisfied yet 72% report major pain points. Users settle for "good enough" — they're not loyal, just lacking alternatives.
72% dual paradox rate
04
THREAT
Kirana Store Is The Real Competitor
38% cite "high prices vs local stores" — users mentally benchmark every purchase against neighborhood shops. Convenience premium has a limit.
38% price-sensitive users
05
GAP
Blinkit's Expectation-Reality Gap
Users choose Blinkit for variety but complain about stockouts. The promise vs delivery mismatch creates higher disappointment than competitors.
34% cite availability issues
06
HIDDEN
Daily Users ≠ Homogeneous Group
Daily users have the widest spending variance (₹200 to ₹1,200 AOV). Two distinct sub-segments hidden under "power user" label — essentials buyers vs grocery consolidators.
6x spend variance in daily users

🎯 Business Recommendations

FOR BLINKIT
Fix Availability Before It Becomes Churn
Implement real-time "availability promise" + predictive inventory. Your users choose you for variety — stockouts break trust irreversibly.
FOR ZEPTO
Launch "Local Price Match" for 50 Essentials
Price daily essentials within 5% of kirana rates. Absorb margin, recover through impulse add-ons. Convert bi-weekly users to daily.
FOR ALL APPS
Micro-Loyalty: Reward Habits, Not Spend
47% are passives. Streak-based loyalty (3-day, 7-day bonuses) borrowed from gaming psychology can convert passive → promoter.
